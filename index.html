Cheat Sheet & Actions

Quick references, lists, and takeaways extracted from the core chapters of the book.

1. Password Rules (Ch 7)
  • Use complex phrases, not words (e.g. correct horse battery staple).
  • Use unique passwords for each service (prevent credential stuffing).
  • Never reuse passwords on personal and business accounts.
  • Store passwords securely using a credential manager application.
2. Safe Public Wi-Fi (Ch 20)
  • Turn off auto-connect to prevent joining dummy networks.
  • Avoid sensitive transactions (online banking/shopping) on public Wi-Fi.
  • Use a Virtual Private Network (VPN) for encrypted tunnels.
  • Use cellular hotspot data rather than public hotspots where possible.
3. Backup Rules (Ch 13)
  • Use the 3-2-1 backup strategy (3 copies, 2 media types, 1 off-site/cloud).
  • Ensure external storage drives are disconnected after backup completes.
  • Test restoring from backups regularly.
  • Verify files are encrypted on cloud servers.
4. Breach Recovery (Ch 12 & 15)
  • Stay calm, act fast, and isolate the infected system from network.
  • Clear the active malware threat before restoring backups.
  • Report critical business data breaches to regulators immediately.
  • Change credentials for all linked accounts immediately.
5. Social Engineering (Ch 8)
  • Never open attachments in unexpected emails.
  • Do not trust caller ID; verify identity through official phone numbers.
  • Limit personal information shared on social networks.
  • Double-check domain names in hyperlinks before clicking.
6. Business Security (Ch 9 & 10)
  • Assign clear responsibility of cybersecurity to one manager or CISO.
  • Educate employees regularly on social engineering and password habits.
  • Carry out thorough cybersecurity audits during company acquisitions.
  • Implement role-based access control policies.
